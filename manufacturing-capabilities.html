Production capability

Manufacturing certainty starts before cutting

Buildability, materials, and inspection are resolved as one production system.

Reliable furniture production depends on more than machinery. Drawing control, material selection, tolerances, assembly logic, samples, and inspection criteria must all point to the same approved result.

Our design-for-manufacture review converts the project brief into coordinated panel parts, metalwork, upholstery, hardware, and installation interfaces.

For repeated room types or units, controlled CNC files and schedules establish a consistent baseline while still allowing configured dimensions and finishes.

Production technologies

The right process at every interface.

Digital accuracy is carried through edging, framework fabrication, assembly, and finishing so components meet cleanly on site.

01

CNC Precision Routing

Digitally controlled cutting, drilling, pocketing, and profiling for repeat panel geometry, hardware positions, service openings, and nesting efficiency.

  • Panel optimization and nesting
  • Repeatable cut-outs and fixing points
  • Component coding by room or unit
02

Edge Banding

Machine-applied edge protection creates consistent adhesion, clean corners, and sealed panel edges for high-contact and moisture-prone applications.

  • PVC and ABS edge options
  • Matched or contrasting specifications
  • Controlled finishing and inspection
03

Welded Frameworks

Steel and aluminum structures support beds, desks, counters, tables, seating, and other assemblies where rigidity and span matter.

  • Jigs for repeat geometry
  • Ground and dressed weld preparation
  • Powder-coated finish coordination
04

Assembly & Upholstery

Hardware installation, carcass assembly, foam build-up, cover fabrication, and final fit checks bring panel, metal, and soft components together.

  • Dry-fit and first-article review
  • Commercial foam and cover options
  • Protected packing by delivery sequence

Specialized material library

Selected against weight, wear, moisture, and finish intent.

Final products and certifications are proposed against the project specification, approved samples, availability, and required supplier documentation.

SURFACE / 01

HPL Laminates

High-pressure laminate surfaces offer broad colour and texture options with dependable resistance to cleaning, abrasion, and daily contact.

Best for
Worktops, carcasses, lockers, counters
Options
Matte, woodgrain, solid, anti-fingerprint

CORE / 02

Lightweight Ply

Poplar and paulownia-based plywood help control furniture dead load while maintaining practical screw holding and machining performance.

Best for
Caravan cabinetry, beds, storage
Options
Thickness, face grade, laminate build-up

UPHOLSTERY / 03

Marine-Grade Vinyl

Cleanable upholstery options support fixed seating, headboards, and welfare furniture exposed to humidity and frequent maintenance.

Best for
Booths, benches, panels, headboards
Options
Colour, texture, performance specification

STRUCTURE / 04

Powder-Coated Steel

Fabricated steel frames provide rigid support for long spans, heavy loads, repeated movement, and replaceable panel or upholstery assemblies.

Best for
Bed frames, desks, tables, counters
Options
Section, colour, texture, corrosion system

Design for manufacture

Resolve risk on screen and in the prototype, not during rollout.

01

Interface review

Check wall, floor, door, window, MEP, equipment, access, and structural fixing conditions.

02

Production detailing

Define assemblies, tolerances, edge treatments, hardware, component codes, and installation sequence.

03

Sample approval

Align colour, texture, edge, hardware, upholstery, and workmanship through finish samples or a first article.

04

Controlled release

Freeze approved information before batch production and record project-authorized revisions.
